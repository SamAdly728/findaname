How is Domain Value Calculated?

A domain's value is influenced by many factors. Our AI model considers these key attributes to provide a data-driven estimate, but remember that the final sale price can vary based on negotiation and market demand.

  • Top-Level Domain (TLD): Extensions like .com are typically the most valuable, followed by country-specific (ccTLDs) and newer generic TLDs like .io and .ai.
  • Keywords & SEO: Domains containing high-value, high-traffic keywords are more desirable and easier to rank in search engines.
  • Length & Memorability: Shorter, catchy, and easy-to-spell domains are more brandable and therefore more valuable.
  • Age & History: Older domains that have a clean history (no spam) are often seen as more trustworthy by search engines.
  • Commercial Potential: The most important factor is its potential to be monetized, whether as a brand, an e-commerce store, a blog, or a SaaS product.
